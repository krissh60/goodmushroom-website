When a DPR is useful

A DPR is useful once you have moved beyond a broad idea. You may know the species, location or intended product format, but need a clear commercial framework that can be discussed with partners, advisers or internal stakeholders.

It is not a shortcut to a loan, subsidy, buyer, licence or profitable result. Those outcomes depend on independent institutions, execution and market conditions. The job of the report is to make the underlying project more legible and testable.

What a DPR does not include by default

A DPR does not automatically include buyer introductions, loan or subsidy approval, licence applications, construction supervision, vendor appointment, live operational management or a promise that a projected financial outcome will occur. A requirement outside the fixed scope is discussed and priced in writing first.

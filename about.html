Our Operations

What We Do, Exactly

We are a full-service mushroom trading and export company — not just a marketplace.

🏔️ 🌾 🍄

Sourcing & Farming

We grow Cordyceps militaris, Shiitake, and Lion's Mane at our own facility in Himachal Pradesh — taking advantage of the ideal high-altitude climate. We also buy from a network of verified partner farms across India, visiting each farm to assess quality and growing practices.

We do not simply list products on a platform. We physically visit farms, sign offtake agreements, and build long-term sourcing relationships that give buyers a predictable and dependable supply.

🏔️ Own cultivation facility — Himachal Pradesh
🌾 Partner farms across 10+ Indian states
🔍 Farm audit and verification protocol
🔬 📦 ✈️

Processing & Export

Fresh mushrooms are processed — cleaned, graded, dried or powdered — at our processing unit. Extracts are produced using standardised dual-extraction methods with third-party lab testing for every batch.

We handle all export requirements: FSSAI licensing, APEDA registration, phytosanitary certification, IEC compliance, commercial invoicing, packing lists, and coordination with freight forwarders for both air and sea cargo.

🔬 NABL-accredited lab testing
📋 Full export documentation support
✈️ Air & sea freight coordination

Why Mushrooms?

A Market Whose Time Has Come

The global medicinal mushroom market is projected to grow from USD 9 billion in 2026 to over USD 22 billion by 2032. Demand for Cordyceps, Lion's Mane, and Reishi extracts is exploding in the US, EU, and East Asian markets.

India — with its diverse climates, skilled farmers, low production costs, and massive growing capacity — is perfectly positioned to become a dominant global supplier. We intend to lead that transition.

Himachal Pradesh Advantage

High Altitude = High Quality

Himachal Pradesh's cool temperatures, clean air, and pristine water make it one of the best places in India to cultivate high-potency medicinal mushrooms like Cordyceps — with bioactive concentrations comparable to Tibetan-sourced varieties at a fraction of the price.
